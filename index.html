About ACM

ACM, the Association for Computing Machinery, is an international scientific and educational organization dedicated to advancing the arts, sciences, and applications of information technology. With a world-wide membership of 80,000, ACM functions as a locus for computing professionals and students working in the various fields of Information Technology.

Joining the ACM as a student provides access to many benefits. Here are just a few:

  • Free access to software through the ACM Student Academic Initiative
  • Access to online courses, online books, and webinars
  • Electronic subscription to Communications of the ACM
  • The option to subscribe to the full ACM Digital Library

Fees vary depending on the membership level you purchase, starting at $19. (You may also have access to free student memberships through participation in ICPC or membership in the Upsilon Pi Epsilon honor society.) For more information, including a full list of benefits and membership options, check out http://www.acm.org/membership/student.

Our Chapter

The Radford University student chapter of the ACM is for any student interested in computers, programming, or other aspects of information technology. We focus on organizing events like workshops, tech talks, and video game tournaments. There are no club dues or formal membership requirements. To get involved, just follow our page on OrgSync / RUInvolved where you can see all the latest news and events.
